# Group By ROLLUP

## 🔹 What is ROLLUP?

- ROLLUP is an extension of GROUP BY that creates multiple levels of grouping in a single query.
- It is mainly used for subtotals and grand totals.
- Think of it as automatically adding “super-aggregate” rows to your grouped results

## Syntax

```sql
SELECT column1, column2, aggregate_function(column3)
FROM table_name
GROUP BY ROLLUP (column1, column2);
```

## 🔹 Example

Suppose we have a sales table:

```sql
id | region   | product   | amount
---+----------+-----------+--------
 1 | North    | Laptop    | 50000
 2 | North    | Phone     | 20000
 3 | South    | Laptop    | 45000
 4 | South    | Tablet    | 30000
 5 | North    | Laptop    | 55000
 6 | South    | Phone     | 25000
```

Query:

```sql
SELECT region, product, SUM(amount) AS total_sales
FROM sales
GROUP BY ROLLUP(region, product);
```

Output:

```sql
 region | product | total_sales
--------+---------+------------
 North  | Laptop  | 105000
 North  | Phone   | 20000
 North  | NULL    | 125000   -- subtotal for North
 South  | Laptop  | 45000
 South  | Tablet  | 30000
 South  | Phone   | 25000
 South  | NULL    | 100000   -- subtotal for South
 NULL   | NULL    | 225000   -- grand total
```

## 🔹 How it works

- (region, product) → Regular grouping
- (region) → Subtotal for each region
- () → Grand total for all rows

## 📘 Adding SUBTOTAL and GRAND TOTAL with ROLLUP

```sql
SELECT
    COALESCE(region, 'ALL REGIONS') AS region,
    CASE
        WHEN region IS NOT NULL AND product IS NULL THEN 'SUBTOTAL'
        WHEN region IS NULL AND product IS NULL THEN 'GRAND TOTAL'
        ELSE product
    END AS product,
    SUM(amount) AS total_sales
FROM sales
GROUP BY ROLLUP(region, product);
```

## Output

```sql
   region     |   product    | total_sales
--------------+--------------+------------
 North        | Laptop       | 105000
 North        | Phone        | 20000
 North        | SUBTOTAL     | 125000
 South        | Laptop       | 45000
 South        | Tablet       | 30000
 South        | Phone        | 25000
 South        | SUBTOTAL     | 100000
 ALL REGIONS  | GRAND TOTAL  | 225000
```

## 🔹 When to Use ROLLUP?

- Generating reports with subtotals and totals.
- Summarizing data at different levels without writing multiple queries.
- Useful in business intelligence & analytics queries.

✅ Tip: Use COALESCE() to replace NULL with labels like "TOTAL" or "SUBTOTAL" for cleaner reports.
