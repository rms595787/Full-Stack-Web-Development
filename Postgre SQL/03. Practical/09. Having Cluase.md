# Having Clause

- The HAVING clause is used in SQL to filter groups of rows after the GROUP BY operation.
- Unlike the WHERE clause (which filters individual rows before grouping), HAVING works on aggregate results (like SUM, AVG, COUNT, etc.).

## Syntax

```sql
SELECT column1, aggregate_function(column2)
FROM table_name
GROUP BY column1
HAVING condition;
```

## 🔹 Example

Suppose we have a sales table:

```sql
id | product   | quantity | price
---+-----------+----------+------
 1 | Laptop    |    2     | 50000
 2 | Phone     |    5     | 20000
 3 | Laptop    |    3     | 50000
 4 | Tablet    |    4     | 30000
 5 | Phone     |    6     | 20000
```

Find products with total quantity sold greater than 5:

```sql
SELECT product, SUM(quantity) AS total_qty
FROM sales
GROUP BY product
HAVING SUM(quantity) > 5;
```

Output:

```sql
product | total_qty
--------+----------
Phone   | 11
Laptop  | 5
```

## 🔹 Difference: WHERE vs HAVING

| Clause     | Works On                           | Example Usage              |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| **WHERE**  | Individual rows (before grouping)  | `WHERE price > 20000`      |
| **HAVING** | Aggregated groups (after grouping) | `HAVING SUM(quantity) > 5` |

✅ Tip: Always use HAVING with aggregate functions. If you just want to filter rows, use WHERE.
